# Term life — best-practice reference

Pure protection for dependents. Read with `reference.md`. Interview, scoring, and Q&A on term policies all draw on this.

## Interview — what to ask (one at a time)

Age · annual income · dependents + their ages · outstanding loans (home/auto/personal) · existing life cover · smoker/tobacco · target retirement age. Also useful: existing liquid corpus (nets down the goals method), any known health conditions (affects insurability + premium honesty).

Missing → stated assumption in the Assumptions table (e.g. retirement age 60, education corpus ₹50L/child).

## Cover sizing (feeds `insurance_math.term_cover_need`)

Take the **larger** of:
- **Income replacement**: 10–15× annual income (use 15× when dependents are young, 10× near retirement).
- **Liability + goals**: outstanding loans + children's education corpus + (annual family expenses × years to youngest dependent's independence) − existing liquid corpus.

Cover **till expected retirement (~60)**, not till 85 — whole-of-life term is poor value. The gap = need − existing cover.

## Scoring checklist (weighted — score each candidate, higher = better)

| Criterion | Weight | What good looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **Claim settlement ratio (IRDAI)** | high | ≥ 97% by count, cross-checked with **amount-settlement ratio** (count alone hides big-ticket rejections) |
| **Solvency ratio** | high | comfortably above IRDAI's 1.5 floor |
| **Premium for the cover** | high | competitive per ₹1 Cr for the age/term; healthy 30-something ≈ ₹12–18k/yr per ₹1 Cr |
| **Claim experience / repudiation reputation** | med | few disputed-rejection complaints; ombudsman record clean |
| **Premium-paying-term flexibility** | med | regular-pay usually best value; limited-pay only if it genuinely fits cashflow |
| **Rider quality** | med | see below — worth-taking vs gimmick |
| **Insurer stability / vintage** | low | established book, no solvency scares |

## Riders — worth it vs gimmick

- **Worth considering:** accidental death/disability, **waiver-of-premium** (premiums waived if you're disabled), critical-illness only if you lack separate cover.
- **Usually gimmicks:** **return-of-premium** (a bad FD bolted on — inflates premium massively for near-zero real return), income-payout variants that just re-package the sum assured.

## Red flags

- Recommending an endowment/ULIP/"guaranteed return" plan when the need is pure protection — the classic mis-sell.
- Pressure to under-declare smoking or a condition to lower premium — that's the future claim rejection.
- Very long premium-paying-term or cover-till-99 framed as "value".
- Medical test waived on a large cover for an older applicant — non-medical underwriting raises later-repudiation risk.
- CSR quoted by count only, hiding a weak amount-settlement ratio.

## Sizing sanity (2026 metro context)

Term life for a healthy 30-something ≈ ₹12–18k/yr per ₹1 Cr, rising with age and for smokers. A quote far outside that band for a standard profile is worth questioning.
