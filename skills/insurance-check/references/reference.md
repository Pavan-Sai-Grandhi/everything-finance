# Insurance knowledge base

Grounded in Zerodha Varsity — Insurance module (https://zerodha.com/varsity/module/insurance/, 9 chapters by Ditto: Perverse Incentives, The Nudge, Skin in the Game, Dunning-Kruger Effect, A Mighty Defence, No Free Lunch, Gimmick or Not 1–2). Fetched and summarized 2026-06-10.

## Life insurance

- **Term only.** Insurance is protection; mixing it with investment (endowment, ULIP, moneyback) buys bad versions of both. Typical traditional-plan IRR: 4–5%, below inflation. Framework: buy pure term, invest the premium difference per the MF framework.
- **Cover size**: 10–15× annual income as the base rule; refine with liabilities + goals: loans outstanding + education corpus + family expenses till independence − existing assets. Cover till expected retirement age (~60), not till 85 — premiums for whole-of-life terms are poor value.
- **Disclosure is the claim** ("Dunning-Kruger" chapter): smoking, pre-existing conditions, family history — misdeclaration is the #1 cause of claim rejection ("Perverse Incentives" chapter). Never optimize premium via omission.
- Riders worth considering: accidental disability waiver-of-premium. Riders that are usually gimmicks: return-of-premium (it's just a bad FD bolted on).
- Insurer choice: claim settlement ratio (>97% for large insurers) AND amount-settlement ratio; solvency ratio above IRDAI's 1.5 floor.

## Health insurance

Red flags the module hammers ("Skin in the Game", "A Mighty Defence", "No Free Lunch", "Gimmick or Not"):
- **Room-rent caps**: a 1%-of-SI/day cap doesn't just limit the room — proportionate deductions scale down the *entire* bill. Prefer "no room rent limit" or single-private-room.
- **Co-pay**: any mandatory co-pay (common in senior plans) means the insurer keeps skin in *your* game; avoid if possible.
- **Disease sub-limits** (cataract, knee replacement caps) silently gut the cover.
- **Waiting periods**: 2–4y for pre-existing diseases; buy young to serve them while healthy. Porting preserves served waiting periods.
- **Consumables** ("Gimmick or Not 2"): gloves/syringes etc. can be 5–10% of a bill; a consumables rider is one of the few riders worth paying for.
- **Restoration benefit**: full SI restore after a claim within the year — valuable for floaters.
- **Family floater vs individual** ("No Free Lunch"): floater is cheaper but one member's claims deplete everyone's cover; separate policy for senior parents (their premiums and claims would poison the floater).
- **Employer group cover**: real but tied to the job and often capped low — count at half weight; a personal base policy + super top-up is the resilient structure.

## Sizing heuristics (2026 metro context)

- Health: ₹10L individual / ₹15–25L family floater base; super top-up (₹50L–1Cr above a ₹5–10L deductible) costs little and covers the tail.
- Medical inflation 12–14% — a cover that feels generous today halves in real terms in ~6 years.
- Premium sanity: term life for a healthy 30-something ≈ ₹12–18k/yr per ₹1 Cr; health ₹15–25k/yr for a ₹10L floater (rises steeply with age).

## Interview defaults (when the user doesn't state a number)

So the liabilities+goals formula is always computable from a minimal interview — assume, label, and list in the Assumptions table:
- Children's education corpus: ₹40–60L per child in today's money (use ₹50L mid-point)
- Annual family expenses: 40–50% of gross income if unstated
- Endowment bonus accrual: run low/mid/high scenarios (simple reversionary ₹40–50/1000 SA as mid) rather than one guess
